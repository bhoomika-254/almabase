# EduPlatform - FERPA Compliance Policy

## What is FERPA?

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a U.S. federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. FERPA applies to all educational agencies and institutions that receive funding under programs administered by the U.S. Department of Education.

## EduPlatform's FERPA Compliance Status

EduPlatform operates as a "school official" under FERPA when providing its LMS services to covered educational institutions. EduPlatform has a legitimate educational interest in accessing student records solely for the purpose of providing contracted services.

EduPlatform's FERPA compliance has been independently verified by the law firm Greenwald, Davis & Associates LLP (last audit: January 2025).

## Student Rights Under FERPA

EduPlatform's platform supports institutions in upholding the following student rights:

1. **Right to Inspect:** Students have the right to inspect and review their own education records within 45 days of a request.
2. **Right to Request Amendment:** Students may request corrections to inaccurate or misleading records.
3. **Right to Consent:** EduPlatform does not disclose student personally identifiable information (PII) to third parties without written student consent, except as permitted under FERPA.

## Data Access Controls for Student Records

- Only authorized faculty and staff with a verified legitimate educational interest may access student education records.
- Student records are not shared with third parties without explicit written consent from the student or their parent/guardian (for students under 18), except as permitted by FERPA exceptions.
- Administrative access to student data is restricted to EduPlatform employees who require it for platform support, subject to signed Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs).

## Student Data We Collect

EduPlatform collects and stores the following student data on behalf of institutions:

- Name, email address, and institutional ID
- Course enrollment and completion records
- Assignment submissions, grades, and feedback
- Login timestamps and activity logs
- Video lecture viewing history
- Accessibility accommodation flags (where provided by the institution)

## Data That Is NOT Collected

- Social Security Numbers
- Financial account information
- Medical records (unless for accommodation purposes, at institution discretion)
- Biometric data

## Data Retention for Student Records

- **Active enrolled students:** Full data retained for duration of enrollment.
- **Graduated or transferred students:** Data retained for 7 years post-graduation or transfer.
- **Withdrawn or expelled students:** Data retained for 5 years post-withdrawal.
- **Course data (non-identifiable):** Aggregated course analytics retained indefinitely.
- **Backup copies:** Encrypted backups retained for 30 days, then securely deleted.
- **Activity logs:** Audit logs retained for 3 years; security logs retained for 7 years.

## Data Deletion

Upon institution contract termination, student PII is deleted within 90 days following a written deletion request. A certificate of deletion is provided upon completion.

## FERPA Training

All EduPlatform employees who may access student records are required to complete FERPA training annually. Training records are maintained for audit purposes.

## Breach Notification

In the event of a breach involving student education records, EduPlatform will notify the affected institution within 72 hours of confirming the breach. Institutions are then responsible for FERPA-required notifications to students and, where applicable, the Department of Education.
